# Helping Polish GetAdvice 🔎

**GetAdvice is MILO's friendly face — the app real people use to talk to MILO —
and we're always making it better. Your job is simple: USE it, then tell
us what you find.**

You get to be a tester. A tester is a person who tries out an app before
the whole world uses it, then tells the team what worked and what felt
weird. Think of it like a taste-tester for food: you try a bite first and
say "this needs a little more salt" before it goes out to everyone. That
is a real and important job, and you are great at it because you notice
things.

New to this and want the full walkthrough first? Read
[`lessons/07-giving-good-feedback.md`](../lessons/07-giving-good-feedback.md).
It teaches you how to write feedback that is clear and kind. This page is
your quick guide for when you are ready to go.

## How to give feedback

1. Open the template: [`feedback-getadvice/feedback-template.md`](feedback-template.md).
   A template is a fill-in-the-blanks form, like a worksheet.
2. Copy it into a new note.
3. **Make one note per issue or idea.** If you spot three things, that is
   three separate notes. One thing per note keeps each report clear and
   easy to act on.
4. Fill in the blanks with clear, simple steps. Pretend you are telling a
   friend exactly what you did, in order, so they could do the very same
   thing and see the very same result.
5. Add a **screenshot** whenever you can. A screenshot is a picture of your
   screen. It shows the team exactly what you saw, which helps a lot.

If you ever get stuck on a note, you can always ask MILO to help you say it
clearly.

## Small ideas are very welcome

You do not have to find a big broken thing. **Small polish ideas count
just as much.** For example:

- Wording that was confusing or hard to read.
- Spacing that looked cramped or messy.
- A spot that felt slow.
- Anything that just felt a little off.

If it caught your eye, it is worth a note.

## One last thing 💛

Finding problems is **helping**, not criticizing. Every note you write
makes GetAdvice better for the people who use it. The team truly wants to
hear what you found. There are no silly reports here, so tell us everything
you notice.
