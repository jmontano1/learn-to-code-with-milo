# Lesson 1: How the Web Works 🌐

In the last lesson (`lessons/00-start-here.md`) you toured the repo. Nice work. Now let's understand what a web page actually **is**, before you build one.

Every web page you have ever seen — YouTube, a game site, your school portal — is made of just **three** parts working together. Once you can spot those three parts, the whole web stops feeling like magic and starts feeling like something *you* can build.

Still no typing today. Just one idea at a time.

## The house analogy

The easiest way to remember the three parts is to picture building a house.

- **HTML is the structure.** Like the walls, floors, and rooms of a house — the frame that holds everything in place. HTML (short for "HyperText Markup Language," but you do not need to memorize that) is the structure of a page: the headings, the paragraphs, the buttons. It decides *what* is on the page.

- **CSS is the paint and furniture.** They decide how the house *looks*: the colors, the style, whether it feels plain or bold. CSS decides how the page looks: the colors, the fonts, the spacing. Same house, a whole new look.

- **JavaScript is the electricity.** The wiring makes things *happen* when you flip a switch. JavaScript makes the page *do* things. When you click a button and something happens, that is JavaScript.

Notice the order. First you build the structure. Then you paint and furnish it. Then you wire it up so things work. That is exactly the order you will build in.

## Quick reference table

| Part | House analogy | Its job | Example |
|------|--------------|---------|---------|
| HTML | Structure 🏗️ | Structure: *what* is on the page | A heading that says "Hello" |
| CSS | Paint & furniture 🎨 | Style: *how* it looks | Making that heading blue |
| JavaScript | Electricity ⚡ | Behavior: *what it does* | A button that shows a message when clicked |

Keep this table in your back pocket. For the rest of these lessons, whenever you look at a page, you can quietly ask yourself: "Is this structure, style, or wiring?"

## Your task: see all three at once

Let's watch the three parts team up in one tiny example. You will not type anything yet. You are just going to *look*, and let MILO point out which part is which.
