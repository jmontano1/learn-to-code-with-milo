# 🔥 The Forge — your Level 4 workspace

This is where you stop building projects and start forging an empire.

Everything you make in **Level 4 (The Forge)** lives here. Unlike the rest of `my-projects/`, this folder is not for practice — it's for real things, with real users, real stakes, and your name on them. Treat it that way.

## The laws you build under

Every room in this forge is judged against the three ideas at the heart of MILO:

- **Adaptive Orchestration** — your systems bend when the world changes, instead of shattering.
- **Supervisory Primacy** — you can always see, stop, and answer for anything your system does. Power keeps its reins.
- **Viability** — it only counts if it survives contact with the real world: real users, real load, real money.

## Your forge log

Below, keep a running log of the one real thing you're forging into an empire across Level 4 — what it is, who it's for, and why it deserves to exist. Update it as the fire reshapes the plan. This is the first page of your founder's story.

> **What I'm forging:** _(write it here)_
>
> **Who it's for:** _(one real person, not "everyone")_
>
> **Why it deserves to exist:** _(three sentences a stranger would nod at)_

**Say it out loud, on camera.** Record a 30-second phone video — your face, your voice, your real room — saying the one thing you're forging and why *you personally* want it to exist. Keep it in this folder (`my-projects/forge/my-goal.mp4`). No AI can fake your face, your voice, and your city; this is the truest thing in the whole workspace, and your instructor will hold your final work up against it. If you shipped a generic to-do app when the video said "this is for a real salon down the street," that gap is the first thing they'll ask about.

**Pick your stake.** Choose a small wager *you* set, and tell your instructor out loud — but shape it around *defending* the work, not around the app merely existing: **"If by Sunday I can explain every part of this to my instructor with the files open, I earn it. If I can't, honest redo, no reward."** The prize isn't that it runs. It's that you can prove it's yours.
