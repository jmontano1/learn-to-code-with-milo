# Words explained simply

Coding has a lot of new words. That can feel like walking into a room where everyone speaks a language you do not know yet.

Do not try to memorize this page. Just come back to it whenever a word shows up and you think, "Wait, what does that mean again?" That is exactly what it is for. 🙂

The words are grouped so related ideas sit near each other.

---

## Where your work lives

**File**
One document on your computer, like a single page in a notebook. A file has a name and it holds one thing, such as your web page or your notes.

**Folder**
A container that holds files, like a drawer that holds many papers. Folders keep your files tidy so you can find them later. Folders can hold other folders too.

**Repository**
The whole project folder that git watches over. People shorten it to "repo." Think of it like a magic box that remembers every version of everything you have ever put inside it, so nothing is truly lost.

---

## Saving and sharing your work

**Commit**
A saved snapshot of your work at one moment. Think of it like a photo of your project as it looks right now. Later you can always go back and look at any photo you took.

**Push**
Sending your saved commits from your computer up to the internet, usually to GitHub. Think of it like uploading your photos to a shared album so they are backed up and others can see them.

---

## The three languages of a web page

**HTML**
The language that holds the *content* of a web page: the words, the headings, the buttons. Think of it like the skeleton of a body. It gives the page its shape and its parts, but not its looks yet.

**CSS**
The language that makes a page look nice: colors, sizes, spacing, fonts. Think of it like the clothes and the style on top of the skeleton. Same body, but now it has a look.

**JavaScript**
The language that makes a page *do* things: react to clicks, change text, respond to you. Think of it like the muscles that let the body move. This is what makes a page feel alive.

**Tag**
A small labeled piece of HTML that marks one part of your page, like a heading or a button. Think of it like a sticky note that says "this bit is a title" or "this bit is a paragraph."

---

## Ideas inside code

**Function**
A named set of steps that does one job. Think of it like a recipe. You follow the same steps to get the same result, and you can reuse the recipe any time instead of writing it out again.

**Event**
Something that happens that your code can react to, like a click, a key press, or the page loading. Think of it like a doorbell. When it rings, your code decides what to do next.

**Bug**
A mistake in your code that makes it behave in a way you did not want. Every coder makes them, every single day. Think of it like a typo in a recipe that makes the cake come out wrong. You find it, you fix it, you move on. Bugs are normal, not a sign you are bad at this.

---

## Your tools

**Browser**
The app you use to look at web pages, like Chrome, Safari, or Firefox. Think of it like a window that turns code into the pages you see and click on.

**Claude Code**
Claude living inside your computer, able to read and write your real files and run commands for you. Think of it like a patient coding buddy sitting right next to you, ready to explain anything and help you build. That is the buddy this whole repo teaches you to work with.

---

## Words you'll meet later

You do **not** need these two yet. No lesson so far uses them. They are here just so the word is not a total stranger the day you first bump into it. Skip this bit for now if you like. 🙂

**Variable**
A named box that holds a value you want to remember, like a number or some text. Think of it like a labeled jar. You put something in, write a name on the jar, and grab it later by that name.

**Terminal**
A text window where you type commands to your computer instead of clicking buttons. Think of it like sending short written instructions to your computer, and it writes back. It looks a little scary at first, then it becomes your friend. Good news: in these lessons you save your work by simply asking Claude Code, so you will not need the terminal for a long while.
